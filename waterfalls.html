The City of Waterfalls

Hamilton, Ontario has more waterfalls than any other city in the world per capita. Over 100 cascades cut through the Niagara Escarpment — from thundering curtain falls to hidden ribbon cascades tucked into ancient ravines. This is a city shaped by water and stone.

Hamilton's Most Iconic Waterfalls

From the roaring curtain of Webster's Falls to the towering ribbon of Tew's, each waterfall has its own character, its own trail, and its own story carved into the Niagara Escarpment over thousands of years.

Webster's Falls

📍 Spencer Gorge Conservation Area, Dundas

Hamilton's most iconic waterfall and one of the largest in Ontario. This stunning 22-metre curtain cascade spills over a wide limestone ledge into a plunge pool below, surrounded by ancient Carolinian forest. The accessible boardwalk trail offers multiple viewpoints, and the gorge itself is a geological wonder shaped over 12,000 years. A timed reservation is required during peak season.

22m Height Easy Access Must-See Curtain Falls

Tew's Falls

📍 Spencer Gorge Conservation Area, Dundas

At 41 metres, Tew's Falls is the tallest waterfall in Hamilton and rivals Niagara Falls in height. This dramatic ribbon cascade plunges into the deep Spencer Gorge, and the lookout platform above offers sweeping panoramic views of the Dundas Valley. Connected to Webster's Falls by the Bruce Trail, the hike between the two is one of Hamilton's finest walks. Best visited in spring when snowmelt swells the flow.

41m Height Moderate Tallest in Hamilton Ribbon Falls

Albion Falls

📍 Red Hill Valley, Hamilton Mountain

One of Hamilton's most popular and accessible waterfalls, Albion Falls is a beautiful 19-metre classical cascade that flows over a series of stepped rock ledges. Located in Red Hill Valley, the falls are visible year-round from the lookout above and are particularly stunning after rainfall or during spring thaw. The surrounding trail connects to the broader Red Hill Valley trail system, offering hours of exploration.

19m Height Easy Access Year-Round Cascade Falls

Devil's Punchbowl

📍 Stoney Creek, Hamilton

A spectacular 37-metre ribbon waterfall that plunges into a dramatic horseshoe-shaped gorge carved from ancient Silurian rock. The lookout above offers jaw-dropping views of the layered geological formations and, on clear days, a panorama stretching to Lake Ontario. The rock strata visible in the gorge walls are over 450 million years old. Visit at night for a stunning view of the city lights below.

37m Height Easy Access Dramatic Views Ribbon Falls

Sherman Falls

📍 Ancaster, Hamilton

A beloved hidden gem tucked away in Ancaster's forests. This 17-metre cascade feels like a secret, surrounded by old-growth Carolinian forest and dramatic limestone walls that create a natural amphitheatre. The short trail to the falls follows Ancaster Creek through a quiet, moss-covered ravine. Less crowded than the Spencer Gorge falls, Sherman is a local favourite for those seeking a peaceful waterfall experience.

17m Height Moderate Hidden Gem Cascade Falls

Tiffany Falls

📍 Ancaster, Hamilton

A beautiful 21-metre cascade known across Ontario for its spectacular winter ice formations. When temperatures drop, the falls transform into a cathedral of ice columns and frozen curtains that attract photographers from across the province. In warmer months, the delicate veil of water flows through a quiet Carolinian forest. The short, easy trail from the parking area makes this one of the most accessible waterfalls in Hamilton.

21m Height Easy Access Winter Ice Veil Falls
Plan Your Visit

Know Before You Go

Hamilton's waterfalls are natural treasures. A little planning goes a long way toward a safe and memorable visit.

🌸

Best Seasons

Every season reveals a different side of Hamilton's waterfalls. Spring brings powerful flows from snowmelt — the falls are at their loudest and most dramatic. Fall frames the cascades in brilliant reds, oranges, and golds. Winter transforms many falls into stunning ice sculptures. Summer offers lush green surroundings but draws the biggest crowds — visit early morning on weekdays for the best experience.

⚠️

Safety & Access

Always stay on marked trails and behind safety barriers — escarpment edges can be unstable and slippery. Wear proper footwear with good grip, especially in wet conditions. Some falls like Webster's and Tew's require a timed reservation during peak season through the Hamilton Conservation Authority. Trails may be closed seasonally for safety or ecological restoration. Check conditions before you go.

🚌

Getting There

Many Hamilton waterfalls are accessible by HSR bus, including Albion Falls, Devil's Punchbowl, and Chedoke Falls. Conservation area falls like Webster's and Tew's are best reached by car, with paid parking at Spencer Gorge. For a car-free adventure, the Bruce Trail connects several waterfalls on foot. Hamilton is also accessible from Toronto via GO Transit in under an hour.
